The YouTuber’s Ten Commandments

10 rules that separate channels with 100 viewers from those with millions

💡 Before You Record Your First Video...

Every big channel started from zero. The difference? Creators with millions of subscribers followed these 10 rules from the very beginning. Read them carefully - there’s a quiz coming up!

1

Do Your Research

Before you start recording, learn the topic and watch what other creators are doing. Create something original, not another copy.

2

Think Through Your Topic

Funny or educational? How often will you post videos? Answer these questions BEFORE you create your channel.

3

Stand Out!

YouTube is full of channels that look the same. Do the same thing, but differently. Show your unique point of view.

4

Provide Real Value

People are looking for answers to specific questions. Solve their problems - that’s when they come back for more.

5

Show Your Personality

Viewers come back for the creator, not the topic. Be authentic and don’t copy others - be yourself!

6

Care About Quality

Good sound matters most! A bad microphone puts people off more than a weak camera. Invest wisely.

7

Catchy Titles

“7 Ways to...” beats “Ways to...” every single day. The thumbnail is your channel’s cover - make it good.

8

Interact With Viewers

Reply to comments, ask what they want to see. The better you know them, the better content you’ll create.

9

Create Regularly

The YouTube algorithm loves consistency. One video a week is better than daily for a month, then silence.

10

Social Media

Tell the world you’re making a channel! There’s no point making public videos just to keep them secret.

Resources and Licenses

Where to find legal music, photos, and video for your productions

⚖️ Copyright - Why Does It Matter?

Using someone else’s music, photo, or video without permission is theft of intellectual property - and can get your channel taken down! Luckily, there are plenty of free, legal resources.

🏛️ Creative Commons (CC) License

The creator shares their work under certain conditions. Always check which license applies before using it!

👤
BY - Attribution

You can use it, but credit the author. Commercial use is allowed.

💰
NC - Non-Commercial

For private purposes only - not on a monetized channel.

🔄
SA - Share Alike

Your derivative work must have an identical license.

🚫
ND - No Derivatives

Use only the original - don’t create adaptations.

Setting Up Your YouTube Channel

Step by step - from an empty profile to a finished channel

🚀 Before You Start

You’ll need a Google account. Log in at youtube.com and click your profile icon → Create a new channel.

🏷️ Step 1 - Name and Basic Info ▼

In the form, enter your channel name and accept the terms. Once you’re through the form, you’ll land on an empty channel view - choose Customize channel.

In the Customization → Basic info tab, fill in:

  • Channel name (matching your strategy)
  • Handle / @username
  • Channel description (max 1000 characters - from Google Docs!)
  • Social media links
🎨 Step 2 - Branding and Banner ▼

In the Customization → Branding tab, add:

  • Channel logo - min. 98×98 px, PNG file. Appears next to comments and videos
  • Banner - min. 2048×1152 px. Visible at the top of your channel (you’ll create it in Canva!)
  • Watermark - a logo with a transparent background, shown in the bottom-right corner of videos

💡 Remember to click Publish changes!

📺 Step 3 - Activate Streaming (important!) ▼

Go to Content → Live and click Try it out, then Submit request.

⚠️ Activation can take up to 24 hours! Do this now so you can stream during the next class. You’ll need your phone for SMS verification.

🔧 Step 4 - Channel Settings ▼

Click Settings (bottom of the page). In the Channel → Advanced settings tab:

  • Select “No, channel is not made for kids” - this will allow viewers to comment on videos
  • Add keywords describing your topic (a few, e.g. gaming, Minecraft, Poland)

In Default upload settings → Basic info, set your video visibility and enter basic tags.

✅ Channel Setup Checklist

  • Google account ready, logged into YouTube
  • Channel created, name entered
  • Channel description filled in (from your strategy)
  • Channel logo added
  • Banner created in Canva and added
  • Streaming access request submitted
  • Advanced settings configured
  • Default tags and video visibility set
